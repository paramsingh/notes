Pigeonhole Principle

Book: A Walk through Combinatorics

Pigeonhole principle:

Let nnn and kkk be positive integers and let n>kn \gt kn>k. Suppose we have to place nnn identical balls into kkk boxes. At least one box will need to contain at least 2 balls.

Proof (by contradiction):

Let's assume there is no box with more than 1 ball. Thus each box has either 0 or 1 ball in it. Let the number of boxes with 0 balls be mmm; m≥0m \geq 0m≥0.

Thus, there are k−mk - mk−m boxes which have 1 ball in them.

Thus we've only placed k−mk - mk−m balls into boxes

Now, k−m≤k<nk - m \leq k \lt nk−m≤k<n. Therefore we haven't placed all nnn balls. Hence if we want to place all nnn balls, our initial assumption must be falsified, and we need to have at least one box with more than 1 ball.
