Key Finding: B12 Deficiency → Elevated Homocysteine

The elevated homocysteine (21.20) and low B12 (183) are connected. Homocysteine is metabolized via a B12-dependent pathway (methionine synthase). When B12 is insufficient, homocysteine accumulates.

This matters because:

  1. Homocysteine is an independent cardiovascular risk factor - associated with increased risk of thromboembolic episodes in coronary/cerebral vascular disease
  2. B12 deficiency can cause neurological issues - peripheral neuropathy, weakness, cognitive changes if left untreated long-term
  3. The elevated homocysteine confirms functional deficiency - even though B12 is only slightly below range (183 vs 197), it's low enough to cause metabolic effects

Action Plan

  • Supplement with methylcobalamin 1000-2000 mcg daily (sublingual)
  • Retest B12 and homocysteine in 3 months
  • Both should normalize with supplementation

Vitamin D Insufficiency

Vitamin D at 26.6 ng/mL falls in the "insufficient" range (10-30). Target is 30-100 for sufficiency.

Action Plan

  • Supplement with Vitamin D3 (cholecalciferol) - typical dose 1000-2000 IU daily, or 60,000 IU weekly
  • Get some sun exposure
  • Retest in 3 months
